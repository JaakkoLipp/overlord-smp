**disclaimer this was just used initially and has since changed, but is present for context**


- Soul-link / shared health. All players share a single health pool, or damage is partially distributed across the group. For under 6 people this is transformative: one careless lava step threatens everyone, so it forces genuine coordination and produces constant low-grade tension and comedy. A softer variant is soulbound pairs (players linked two at a time), which seeds alliances and betrayals. Pure datapack, no client mods.


- Revival economy. Permadeath, but the living can resurrect the dead through a costly ritual: a totem plus XP plus rare items at a built structure. Death becomes meaningful without being final, and every death triggers a real group decision ("do we spend our netherite to bring them back, or let them stay out?"). Datapack-able, and a much better stakes mechanic than plain hardcore for a group that wants to keep playing together.


- No natural regeneration + a healing economy. Disable passive regen entirely; healing comes only from golden apples, regen potions, or specific foods. Combat becomes lethal, retreating becomes a real decision, and otherwise-cheap loot suddenly has value. Nearly free to implement (gamerule plus a tweak) and it raises the floor on tension everywhere.


- Quickly escalating difficulty. Mobs scale with total playtime or a rising global "threat level" tied to progression, so the late game stops being trivial. Server-side mods like Scaling Health do this, or a datapack that periodically buffs mob attributes. Good as a background pressure that keeps the endgame honest.


- LLM as an overlord demanding sacrifices. Give the LLM minecrafts admin console via MCP tools (preselect list of commands at first, then moving to free access if works fine) so it can dynamically affect the world based on the flavoring datapacks we can preselect and load. The effects depend on slight buffs to drastic temporary difficulty increases, based on how well the players did.
